Learning Plan

Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

As mentioned in foundation learning plan, I wish to be able to combine my art/design background and my coding knowledge together one day.

In addition, I would like to be able to work remotely. I also want to have the chance to participate in projects that help make the world a better place, such as projects that focus on the rights of women and LGBTQ people, poverty, and environmental issues.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think my idea of my biggest strengths and limitations hasn't changed much throughout Foundations. However, I found a new strength of me which was mentioned in Foundation Reflection was my ability to adjust myself and stay focused on my work during hard times.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I've never been very comfortable with fully expressing myself in front of others as an introvert, and can be a bit reserved when dealing with people I don't familiar with. And as a less confident non-native speaker, public speaking is also a big challenge for me.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

First I'd like to develop my communication skills and even my initiative when interacting with others and working within groups, as I understand how important teamwork is.

Secondly, I'd like to get comfortable with giving and receiving feedback as this is something I'm not very good at, I always find it a bit intimidating when it comes to giving others feedback, especially when this person is more skilled than me.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

As mentioned in my foundation learning plan, I expect them to be friendly, understanding and patient. In addition to that, I'd like to receive feedback regarding both the tech and core learning from the team as I think it helps me to find out the right direction and improve my capability.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I expect myself to be fully engaged in Bootcamp and enjoy the learning journey. I expect to push myself to the limits and I want to be a better version of myself, both in terms of tech and soft skills.

I will not hesitate to seek help from peers or our facilitators if I get stuck for too long. Time management has rarely been an issue in my learning experience, and I am confident that I can manage it well. Regarding work with others, I'll use this quote that I've quoted before and always keep it in mind: "Listen with curiosity, speak with candor, and act with integrity.".

foundation Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

First to get a web developer position at a company that aligns with my values, then I wish to be able to combine my art/design background and my coding knowledge together one day. I would love to try UI/UX design in the future.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

I see myself as a dedicated, disciplined, compassionate individual. Time management is crucial during intensive learning, I believe my strength in discipline could help me to develop good time management and productivity.

Sometimes I can get overly self-critical and that can lead to self-doubting.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to grow my confidence in general. For example, to become more comfortable when working with a group of people that I'm not familiar with yet; become a confident public speaker, etc.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

Since the beginning of the foundation, I have started studying around 9 am every day and have been able to keep a high concentration level for a reasonably long duration each day. I will be respectful and friendly to others, “Listen with curiosity, speak with candor, and act with integrity.”.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

I will be trying to approach our facilitators and others in the group for support or share with people I trust so they can comfort me in any way.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I tend to solve problems by myself using google and youtube, but if I get stuck for too long, I will not hesitate to seek help from the Discord group or our facilitators.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team.

I expect the facilitators to be friendly and understanding, and also be very patient so they won't get too annoyed when I ask some not-that-smart questions.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I have planned to study full time during weekdays, just going to keep some of my routines during the weekend, like some exercise or outdoor activities.
