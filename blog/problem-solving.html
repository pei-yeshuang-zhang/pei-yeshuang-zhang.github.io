Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes

Pseudocode

Pseudocode is something I am trying to do more of, as it helps us break down the complex functions into small bits.

Trying something

This is what I do first when I get stuck, it's always good to give it a go. Don't be afraid of making mistakes, you can always undo.

Rubber ducky method

This method is to explain to an object (like a rubber ducky) the problem you are facing in your code. It's supposed to help you clear your mind and bring clarity to a solution. I haven't tried this method yet because I feel awkward talking to a toy. Guess I just prefer to talk to something that can talk back to me.

Reading error messages

Error messages confused me at the first, but now I found it super useful. It's like a guide that lead me to the right direction.

Console.logging

I use this when I need to check if my code is functioning well. It's a very useful and important method.

Googling

Trust me, I do this A LOT. My tech life would be miserable without Google.

Asking your peers for help

I often discuss problems with a friend of mine who is also studying programming.

Asking coaches for help

I haven't done this yet, but I believe I will if I truly get stuck.

Improving your process with reflection

I'm confident about this one as I use it all the time, I like to go back and see if there are any improvements I can make, and I enjoy the sense of accomplishment I gain when I refactor my code and make it better in any way.
